About Me

My name is Iqbal Ramadhan. I am an experienced researcher and writer with over three years of expertise in politics, public policy, and communications. My work spans qualitative research and policy analysis for government agencies, political figures, and private companies.

Selected Research

Explaining Democratic Backsliding in Indonesia (2019–2024): Structural Conditions and Power Consolidation under Joko Widodo

Master’s Thesis, International University of Japan, 2025
  • Analysed democratic backsliding in Indonesia using a structural approach.
  • Methodology: Qualitative analysis, literature review, and in-depth interviews.
  • Findings: Institutional weakening, executive power consolidation, and opposition/ally’s co-optation.

Survey on Political Dynamics in Depok ahead of the 2024 Election

Lead Researcher, Freelance, 2023
  • Surveyed local political dynamics for DPRD candidates in Depok.
  • Methodology: Online survey and focus group discussion.

Market Survey on Toothpaste Usage Patterns

Researcher, KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2021
  • Survey on toothpaste usage patterns in five major Indonesian cities.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face survey.

Survey on Stunting Prevention Communication

Researcher, KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2021
  • Survey on stunting prevention in seven severely affected regions.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face survey and focus group discussion.
  • Findings: Low awareness of stunting prevention timeline was the main cause of high cases.

Survey & FGDs on Political Dynamics in Java & Madura

Researcher, KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2021
  • Public opinion survey and FGDs with experts in Java and Madura electoral districts.
  • Findings: Urban areas focus on service quality, rural areas on better living standards.

National Survey on the Relocation of the Capital to Kalimantan

Researcher, KedaiKOPI Research Agency, 2019
  • Survey on public opinion about relocating the capital to East Kalimantan.
  • Methodology: Face-to-face interviews.
  • Findings: 39.8% disapproved, 35.6% approved, 24.6% no opinion, while 95.7% respondents in Jakarta said disapproved.
